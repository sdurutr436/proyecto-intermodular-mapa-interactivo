# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We, the Transkarte community, are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We believe in the power of collaborative learning and building educational tools that empower people to explore the world through languages and geography.

We are dedicated to creating an inclusive environment where everyone‚Äîregardless of age, background, gender identity, sexual orientation, ability, ethnicity, or experience level‚Äîfeels respected, valued, and safe to contribute, learn, and grow.

This Code of Conduct applies to all interactions within the Transkarte project, including but not limited to:
- GitHub repositories and issues
- Pull requests and code reviews
- Discussions and forums
- Official events and community gatherings
- Any official communication channels

---

## Expected Behavior

### Be Respectful
- Treat all community members with courtesy and respect
- Listen actively to perspectives different from your own
- Acknowledge and celebrate the contributions of others
- Challenge ideas with evidence and constructive intent, not personal attacks

### Be Inclusive
- Welcome newcomers and help them get started
- Be mindful of language barriers; use clear and accessible communication
- Include diverse perspectives in project decisions
- Create space for underrepresented voices in technology and education

### Be Collaborative
- Work together toward shared goals
- Share knowledge and mentor others when possible
- Give credit where credit is due
- Assume good intent in others' words and actions

### Be Honest and Transparent
- Communicate openly about your capabilities and limitations
- Be transparent about conflicts of interest
- Provide constructive and timely feedback
- Acknowledge mistakes and learn from them

### Be Responsible
- Focus discussions on what benefits the community
- Stay on topic in conversations and issues
- Report harmful behavior to maintainers promptly
- Remember that this is an educational project‚Äîmaintain professional standards

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

We do not tolerate:
- Harassment, bullying, or discrimination of any kind
- Offensive language or insults
- Threats, violence, or intimidating behavior
- Unwelcome romantic or sexual advances
- Spamming, trolling, or deliberately disruptive comments
- Sharing others' private information without consent
- Plagiarism or claiming credit for others' work
- Any form of discrimination based on protected characteristics

This also includes behavior that may seem small or subtle but creates an unwelcoming environment over time.

---

## Reporting and Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by:
1. **Direct Contact**: Reach out to the project maintainers via email or private message
2. **GitHub Issues**: For less sensitive matters, you may open an issue prefixed with `[CODE_OF_CONDUCT]`
3. **Anonymous Reports**: If you prefer anonymity, contact us through a trusted third party or platform

**Important**: All reports will be taken seriously and handled with care and confidentiality.

### Investigation Process

When a report is received:
1. The maintainers will investigate the complaint thoroughly and impartially
2. All parties involved will be given a fair opportunity to respond
3. The reporter will be kept informed of the process and outcome
4. Confidentiality will be maintained to the extent possible

### Consequences

Depending on the severity and context, violations may result in:
- A private warning or constructive conversation
- A temporary or permanent ban from the project
- Removal of inappropriate content
- Escalation to platform authorities if necessary

Any action taken will be proportionate to the violation and aimed at maintaining a safe, respectful community.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces and official communications. It also applies to external spaces and events when:
- You are representing the Transkarte project
- Your behavior directly impacts the project, our community, or individual members

## Our Values

We believe that:
- **Education matters**: Learning should be accessible, engaging, and fun
- **Diversity strengthens us**: Different perspectives lead to better solutions
- **Respect is non-negotiable**: Every person deserves basic human dignity
- **Open collaboration thrives**: When we work together, we achieve more
- **Continuous improvement**: We learn from feedback and grow as a community
